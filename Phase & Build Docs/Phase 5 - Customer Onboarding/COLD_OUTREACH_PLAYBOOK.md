# Cold Lead Outreach Playbook — Med Spa Customer Onboarding (Phase 5)

> **Purpose:** Templates and strategy for contacting med spa clinics you have **no prior
> relationship with** to recruit pilot users. Used in **Phase 5 — Customer Onboarding**
> (after all build phases 1-4 are complete).
>
> **Goal:** Secure 3+ committed pilot clinics from cold outreach.
>
> **Pilot offer:** Free through Month 6. Only ask = 20-30 min feedback call every 2 weeks.
>
> **Advantage:** By Phase 5, the product is complete, tested, and polished — you're
> offering a finished platform, not a work-in-progress.

## Outreach Strategy Overview

### The Multi-Channel Funnel

```
Cold Lead (no relationship)
   │
   ├─ Touch 1: Cold Email (Day 1)
   ├─ Touch 2: LinkedIn Connect (Day 2)
   ├─ Touch 3: Instagram/Facebook Engagement (Day 3-4)
   ├─ Touch 4: Follow-Up Email (Day 5)
   ├─ Touch 5: Cold Call (Day 7)
   └─ Touch 6: Final Email / Breakup (Day 10)
         │
         ▼
   Response → Discovery Call → Pilot Commitment
```

### Key Rules

1. **Personalize the first line.** Reference their clinic name, a service they offer, or
   something specific from their website/social. Generic messages get ignored.
2. **Lead with the pain, not the product.** Med spa owners feel the pain (tool fragmentation,
   no-shows, HIPAA anxiety). Don't pitch features — agitate the pain first.
3. **The word "free" is your hook.** Free through Month 6 is a massive offer. Make it prominent.
4. **Ask for one thing only.** A reply, a call, a demo — never two asks in one message.
5. **Keep it short.** Cold emails should be under 120 words. DMs under 100 words. Calls under
   3 minutes before asking to schedule.
6. **Follow up 3-5 times.** 80% of responses come from touches 3-5. Most people give up after 1.
7. **Always include a soft exit.** "If this isn't relevant, no worries — just let me know."
   This reduces friction and actually increases reply rates.

## Targeting & Lead Sourcing

### Ideal Pilot Profile

| Criterion | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Clinic size** | 1-10 providers (small/medium — owner is decision-maker, no lengthy procurement) |
| **Services** | Mix of injectables (Botox, filler) + wellness (IV therapy, facials, weight loss) |
| **Current software** | Mindbody, Vagaro, Zenoti, or paper/spreadsheet (fragmentation pain is highest) |
| **Owner involvement** | Owner is hands-on, manages daily operations (not absentee owner) |
| **Growth stage** | Growing but frustrated with operational bottlenecks |
| **Location** | Metro areas with 3+ med spas nearby (creates competitive urgency later) |

### Where to Find Cold Leads

| Source | How | What to Collect |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| **Google Maps** | Search "med spa [city]" → filter by rating 4.0-4.7 (not 5.0 — those are complacent) | Clinic name, address, phone, website |
| **Instagram** | Search hashtags: `#medspa`, `#medicalaesthetics`, `#botox`, `#medspaowner`, `#aestheticspractice` | Owner name, IG handle, services offered |
| **Facebook** | Search "med spa" in Pages → filter by local area | Page, phone, website, owner (if listed) |
| **LinkedIn** | Search: "owner" OR "founder" + "med spa" OR "medical aesthetics" + [your region] | Name, title, LinkedIn profile |
| **Industry directories** | AmSpa (American Med Spa Association) directory, MedEsthetics directory | Clinic name, owner, contact info |
| **Yelp / Healthgrades** | Search "med spa [city]" | Reviews (use to identify pain points to reference) |
| **Competitor customer lists** | Check Mindbody/Vagaro bookable business directories | Clinics already using fragmented tools |

### Lead Qualification Quick-Check

Before adding a lead to your outreach list, verify:

- [ ] Owner/practitioner name is findable (for personalization)
- [ ] They have an active website OR Instagram (shows they're tech-receptive)
- [ ] They're in your target metro area (for potential in-person visits)
- [ ] They offer 2+ services (multi-service clinics feel fragmentation more acutely)

## The Core Value Proposition

Every message should hit these **three pillars** in some form:

| Pillar | Message |
|--------|---------|
| **1. Pain acknowledged** | "Scheduling in [Current Software], payments in Square, intake forms in Google Drive — that's 3 tools for one patient." |
| **2. Unified solution** | "One HIPAA-secure portal: scheduling, digital intake, payments, SMS/email reminders, audit logs — all in one place." |
| **3. Risk-free offer** | "Free through Month 6. All I ask is 20-30 min every 2 weeks for your feedback." |

## Channel 5: Cold Call

### Pre-Call Prep

- [ ] Visit their website — note 2-3 specific things to mention
- [ ] Check their Instagram/Facebook — note 1 recent post
- [ ] Know their current software (check if booking widget is Mindbody/Vagaro/Booksy)
- [ ] Have calendar open with 3 available slots to offer
- [ ] Set a timer: goal is to book a demo, not pitch the whole product

### The Call Script

**Opening (10 seconds):**

> "Hi, is this [First Name]? ... Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name]. I'll be quick — I know
> you're busy. I work with med spa owners on operational tools, and I came across
> [Clinic Name]. Do you have 60 seconds?"

**If yes → Pain Probe (30 seconds):**

> "I noticed you're using [Mindbody/Vagaro / "an online booking system"] for scheduling.
> Quick question — are you also using separate tools for patient intake forms and payment
> processing?"

**Listen for:** frustration, confirmation of fragmentation, or "we've been looking for
something better." These are your buying signals.

**If they describe the pain → Value Pitch (30 seconds):**

> "That's exactly what I hear from every clinic owner. I built a portal that puts all of that
> into one HIPAA-secure system — scheduling, digital intake forms with e-signatures, Stripe
> payment tracking, automated SMS reminders to cut no-shows, and full audit logs for staff
> accountability."

**The Ask (15 seconds):**

> "I'm offering it free through Month 6 to a few pilot clinics — I just want your feedback
> every couple of weeks. Can I get 15 minutes on your calendar this week to show you a quick
> demo?"

**Book it immediately:**

> "Great — I have Thursday at 2pm or Friday at 10am. Which works better?"

### Handling Common Phone Responses

| They say | You say |
|----------|---------|
| "I'm too busy right now" | "Totally understand. Can I send a 3-minute video walkthrough you can watch when you have time? What's the best email?" |
| "We already have software" | "That's actually common — most owners I talk to have [Mindbody] but still use Square for payments and Drive for intake. This replaces all three. Worth 15 min to compare?" |
| "Send me an email" | "Absolutely — what's the best address? And I'll follow up with a quick demo video so you can see it in action." (Then send Template A + Loom video) |
| "Is this a sales call?" | "No — I'm not selling anything. I'm looking for pilot feedback on a tool I built, and it's free for the first 6 months. Just want to see if it's useful for real clinics." |
| "I'm not interested" | "No problem at all — thanks for your time, [First Name]. If anything changes, here's my number. Have a great day." (Move on — don't burn the bridge) |

### Voicemail Script (if no answer, max 20 seconds)

> "Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] calling about a free pilot program for med spa
> management software — scheduling, intake, payments, all HIPAA-secure, free for 6 months.
> I'll send a quick email too, but feel free to call me back at [your number]. Thanks!"

## Channel 6: SMS Outreach

> **⚠️ Compliance Note:** SMS marketing has strict legal requirements (TCPA in the US). Only
> text business phone numbers that are publicly listed (not personal mobile numbers obtained
> from lead databases without consent). Include opt-out language. When in doubt, use email/DM
> instead. SMS is best used as a **follow-up** to an email or call where you've already made
> contact, not as a first cold touch.

### SMS Template A — Follow-Up After Email (Day 2 after email, if you have their business line)

> Hi [First Name], it's [Your Name] — I emailed you about the free med spa portal pilot
> (scheduling + intake + payments in one). Wanted to follow up via text. Open to a quick
> call this week? Reply STOP to opt out.

### SMS Template B — Post-Voicemail (same day as missed call)

> Hi [First Name], just left you a voicemail about a free HIPAA-secure portal pilot for
> [Clinic Name]. Scheduling, digital intake, payments — all in one, free for 6 months.
> Want me to send a demo video? Reply STOP to opt out.

### SMS Template C — After Demo Call (follow-up)

> Thanks for the chat today, [First Name]! Here's that demo link: [URL]. I'll follow up
> Monday to see if [Clinic Name] wants to join the pilot. — [Your Name]

## Handling Objections

### "We already use [Mindbody/Vagaro/Booksy]"

> "Totally makes sense — most owners I talk to have a booking system already. The thing is,
> they're usually still using Square for payments and Google Drive for intake on top of it.
> This portal replaces all three in one HIPAA-secure place. And since it's free for 6 months,
> there's no risk in seeing if it's better. Worth 15 minutes?"

### "We're too small / too busy"

> "I actually built this for small clinics specifically — the bigger systems are overkill.
> And the pilot is designed to save you time, not add work. The onboarding takes about 30
> minutes and I handle the setup. The only ongoing ask is a feedback call every couple weeks."

### "Is this really free? What's the catch?"

> "Honestly, the catch is I need feedback. The product is built and tested, but I need real
> clinics using it with real patients to know what to improve. The free period is through
> Month 6 — after that it'd be $150-300/month, but there's no obligation to continue and no
> contract. You can walk away anytime."

### "I need to think about it"

> "Absolutely. What specifically would you want to think through? Happy to answer any
> questions now — or I can send a 3-minute demo video so you can see it before deciding."

### "Send me some information"

> "I'll send a demo video right now — what's the best email? I'll also include a one-pager
> with the key features. And I'll follow up in a few days to answer any questions."

## Tracking & Cadence Rules

### Daily Outreach Targets

| Activity | Daily Target |
|----------|-------------|
| New cold emails sent | 10-15 |
| LinkedIn connection requests | 10-15 |
| Instagram/Facebook DMs | 5-10 |
| Cold calls | 10-15 |
| Follow-up touches (any channel) | 10-20 |

### Weekly Targets

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Total new leads contacted | 40-75 |
| Follow-up sequences active | 30-50 |
| Discovery calls booked | 5-10 |
| Pilots committed (cumulative) | 3 (goal) |

### Expected Conversion Rates

| Stage | Rate | From 100 cold leads |
|-------|------|---------------------|
| Reply / response (any channel) | 15-25% | 15-25 leads |
| Discovery call booked | 5-10% | 5-10 leads |
| Demo completed | 3-7% | 3-7 leads |
| Pilot committed | 2-5% | 2-5 pilots |

> **Bottom line:** To get 3 pilot commitments, plan to contact **60-150 cold leads** across
> all channels. At 10-15 new contacts per day, that's 1-2 weeks of focused outreach.

### Lead Tracking Template

Use a spreadsheet or simple CRM (HubSpot Free, Notion, Google Sheets):

```
| Clinic Name | Owner | Email | Phone | Instagram | Current Software | Channel | Date Contacted | Touch # | Status | Next Action | Next Date | Notes |
|-------------|-------|-------|-------|-----------|-----------------|---------|---------------|---------|--------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Glow Aesthetics | Sarah | sarah@... | 512-... | @glowaesth | Mindbody | Email | Jun 14 | 1 | Sent | Follow-up email | Jun 19 | Mentioned no-show pain in review |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
```

**Status values:** `Sent` → `Opened` → `Replied` → `Call Booked` → `Demo Done` → `Pilot Yes` → `Pilot No` → `Not Interested` → `Follow Up Later`

### Cadence Rules

1. **Never contact the same lead on the same day via 2+ channels** (feels spammy). Spread
   touches across different days.
2. **Wait at least 2 days between touches** on the same channel.
3. **Stop after 6 touches** (the breakup email) unless they ask you to follow up later.
4. **Always log the next action + date** so nothing falls through the cracks.
5. **Re-engage "Follow Up Later" leads** after 30-60 days with a new angle or feature update.
6. **Reply within 2 hours** when a cold lead responds — momentum is everything.
